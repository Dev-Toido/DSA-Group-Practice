# 🧠 The O(1) Club: Solving Guide & Protocol

Welcome to the grind. To make sure we actually learn logic and don't just burn out staring at errors, every member must follow this solving protocol. 

## ⏱️ 1. The Time-Box Rule
* **Target:** Complete the agreed-upon questions for the session (e.g., 2 questions).
* **Time Limit:** Spend a maximum of **45 to 60 minutes** on a single question. 
* **Breakdown:** 15 mins thinking/paper math, 15 mins coding, 15-30 mins debugging.
* **The Hard Stop:** If you are stuck for 1 hour and it is still broken, **STOP CODING.** Do not spend 3 hours on one loop. Move to the "Stuck Protocol."

## 🚧 2. The "Stuck" Protocol (WIP Commits)
If you hit the 1-hour mark and your code is failing:
1. Add a comment exactly where you are confused. Example: `// STUCK HERE: Why does this loop skip the last digit?`
2. Save the file and stage it.
3. Commit it with a WIP (Work In Progress) tag. Example: `git commit -m "WIP: Stuck on Question 8"`
4. Push it to your folder. 
5. Bring it to our next standup meeting. We will debug it together.

## 🤝 3. The Squad Standup (Google Meet)
We hold regular 30-minute sync-ups to review our recent progress.
* **Format:** We use Google Meet. Be ready to share your screen.
* **Agenda:**
    * Quick status check (Who hit their targets? Who got stuck?).
    * Screen share and debug the WIP commits together.
    * Review the successful codes to see who had the best approach.

## 🏆 4. The Official Vault
We do not provide answer keys before you try. 
At the end of our standup meetings, the group will vote on whose solution was the cleanest and most efficient. That person will copy their solution into the `Official_Vault/` directory. This creates a master study guide built entirely by the squad.

---
*Remember: Writing bad code and fixing it is how you learn. Let's get to work.*
